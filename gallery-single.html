WHY (Y) 

 

Project Y(WHY) is a parody an e-commerce website that satirizes the recent changes made to Twitter(X) under Elon Musk’s new leadership. Just like X, Project Y “sells” features typical to social media platforms, including the blue tick mark, highlighting the absurdity of placing everyday interactions behind a paywall. These decisions can be a threat that encourages other social media platforms to monetize basic tools. This may change social media platforms where accessibility becomes contingent on affordability.

Y (Why Monetize Social Media?)

This social commentary also touches on some questions about a modern problem with social media: Who decides what is credible or newsworthy? The act of selling “Free Speech Pro” and “Post Boost” as a product is a direct jab at this question. In the world of Project Y, the people who control credibility and newsworthiness are the owners of the platform. However, since they are only interested in profit, Y’s subscription model would only amplify the voices of the wealthy while limiting the accessibility of social media to the rest. It’s easy to see how these hefty subscriptions may transform Y into a platform where only the rich have a say in matters that should be open to everyone. It loses its ability to foster a shared experience by constructing hierarchical systems and goes against the ethos of an inclusive online space. It also undermines the concept of social media and diminishes user trust as it will be viewed as a profit-centric platform. In this capitalist world of ‘Elonomics’, even basic human rights, like free speech, are exclusive.

By using inspired design and branding features from Twitter, this project imitates a plausible online store for the different elements Elon Musk is trying to sell to his X users. After interacting with the project, we want the audience to find the absurdity and humor in these changes for X’s monetary gain. After all, shouldn’t they be using our internet data to make as much money as possible from advertisements? In the end, no matter what Musk’s reasoning for this new payment model, time will tell whether the subscription model is successful or not; whether it changes the engagement of the platform for companies or users.

(This project was a pair project where the work was eaqually divided. A skeleton template was taken from bootstrap and changes were made for it to resemble the brand identity of X)
